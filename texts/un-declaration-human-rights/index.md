# Universal Declaration of Human Rights

**Adopted**: December 10, 1948
**Location**: Paris, France
**Adopting Body**: United Nations General Assembly (Resolution 217 A)

The Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR) is a milestone document in the history of human rights. Drafted by representatives with different legal and cultural backgrounds from all regions of the world, the Declaration was proclaimed by the United Nations General Assembly in Paris on 10 December 1948 as a common standard of achievements for all peoples and all nations.

The UDHR sets out, for the first time, fundamental human rights to be universally protected and has been translated into over 500 languages. It consists of a preamble and 30 articles setting forth the human rights and fundamental freedoms to which all people are entitled.

## Key Themes

The declaration addresses:
- Civil and political rights (Articles 1-21)
- Economic, social, and cultural rights (Articles 22-27)
- The framework for a just society (Articles 28-30)

## Historical Context

The UDHR was drafted in the aftermath of World War II as a response to the atrocities committed during the war. The document represents the first global expression of rights to which all human beings are inherently entitled.

## Public Domain Status

The UDHR is freely available and may be reproduced and distributed at no cost. Member states were called upon to publicize and disseminate the text widely.
